StreetProof+

StreetProof+ is an optional upgrade that turns your map into a game and adds a few power features:

  • Proof Pins: every block in your cities gets a pin. Run the street and the pin is yours; watch your map fill in run by run.
  • Progress by city: completion percentages, pin counts, and a coverage map for every city you're working on.
  • Scan & Suggest: run somewhere new, and a quick scan notices and offers to add that city's pins.
  • Custom routes: map out new ground with your pins and past runs in view, then follow along live on your phone or watch.
  • Spoken splits: mile and pace in your ears, no glancing at your wrist.
  • GPX export: take your runs with you, including ones imported from Apple Health.

Custom routes are for planning your run before you head out the door. Open Menu → Custom Routes and draw right on the map: tap to extend the route, drag to reshape it, and undo if you change your mind. The total distance updates as you draw, so you can build exactly the miles you want, and your pins and past runs stay in view while you plan.

Saved routes sync to your watch and appear as a map overlay during a run on either device, so you can follow along as you go. On the watch, choose which routes show in Settings → Custom Routes.

Two options: $4.99 a year with a 7-day free trial, or $19.99 once for lifetime access with no subscription at all. You'll find the upgrade anywhere you see the StreetProof+ badge in the app; Menu → Progress is the easiest place to start.

The trial runs on the yearly plan: everything unlocks free for 7 days, and you're only charged when the trial ends. If it's not for you, cancel any time before then in your iPhone's Settings (tap your name → Subscriptions) and you won't pay a thing.

You never pay twice. Your purchase is tied to your Apple ID: open Menu → Progress, tap Get StreetProof+, then tap Restore Purchases. The app unlocks and runs a short setup that scans your run history and lights your map back up. Your Proof Pin progress is rebuilt from your workouts, so nothing is lost.

App Store purchases are billed by Apple, so refunds go through Apple too. Sign in at reportaproblem.apple.com, find the purchase, and request a refund. Apple reviews and decides these directly.

Proof Pins & Progress

Proof Pins are the heart of StreetProof+. Every block in your cities gets a pin at its center. Run the street and the pin flips from uncompleted to completed. The proof is yours, and when you've covered them all, so is the city.

Pins are dropped by algorithm, not scouted on foot. Use your head out there: skip any pin you can't reach safely, stay alert, and stay visible.

Open Menu → Progress and tap the + (or go through Menu → Map Options → Manage Cities). On the Add tab, search for a city. If its pins are ready, tap Download and you're set. If we haven't built that city yet, tap Request and we'll place its pins for you; new cities can take some time, and yours appears automatically when it's ready. StreetProof covers US cities and towns, from the biggest cities down to small unincorporated communities.

Scan & Suggest does the searching for you: it looks through your run history, finds the cities you've been running that don't have pins yet, and lets you add each one with a tap.

Auto-Add Cities saves you a step. When it's on, finishing a run in a new area automatically adds that city's Proof Pins to the app, so your Progress page and pin stats in Workout Details just update on their own. No need to open Manage Cities or search for it yourself.

It's on by default for StreetProof+ users. If you'd rather add cities by hand, toggle it off in Menu → Map Options. You can still add any city manually through Map Options → Manage Cities or the + button on the Progress page.

Menu → Progress is your coverage, city by city: each row shows the city's completion percentage, how many pins you've completed, and a mini-map of your coverage so far. Finish a city and it moves to your completed shelf up top. Tap any city for the full picture: a coverage map of the whole city, plus pins remaining, runs, and distance there.

Open any run from Menu → History and check the Metrics tab. The Proof Pins card shows the pins that run completed, plus its Explore Score: a ring showing how much of the run was new ground (green) versus streets you'd already proofed (blue). A high score means you were out exploring; a low one means a familiar loop. Both count.

Pins are placed by algorithm, so occasionally one lands somewhere that doesn't make sense. You can flag it right from the map, and your feedback helps us make the packs better for everyone.

With Proof Pins visible on the map, tap Pin Feedback in the bottom-right corner. Confirm you'd like to report a pin, then select a reason:

  • Temporarily closed (e.g., construction)
  • Private or closed road
  • Not safe to run here
  • Street doesn't exist

Tap Submit and you're done. The reported pin is shaded on your map while we review the report. If we confirm the issue, the pin is dropped from the publicly available pack so no one else runs into it. We really appreciate the help keeping the pins accurate.

Troubleshooting

StreetProof shows workouts from Apple Health that include GPS route data. Workouts without route data (for example, indoor workouts or workouts from apps that don't save routes to Health) won't appear on the coverage map.

If a workout still isn't showing, open Menu → History and tap the refresh button, and make sure StreetProof has all the permissions it asks for. You can check them in Menu → Support; changes are made in the iPhone's Settings app under Privacy & Security.

Still missing? See the next question for a full resync.

Open Menu → Support and tap Resync All from HealthKit. It re-checks Apple Health for every workout with a route and imports anything that's missing. It's safe to run: existing workouts are preserved, and nothing is deleted.

Open Menu → Support and tap Reprocess All Proof Pins. It rebuilds your coverage from scratch from your workout history and selected activity types, so your map and Progress numbers come back correct. It's safe to run, and nothing is deleted.

A few reasons a place might not be available:

  • Proof Pins are currently US-only, covering cities, towns, and communities across all 50 states, DC, and Puerto Rico.
  • Some places don't have clearly defined boundaries in the public map data we use to build pin packs. If we can't find a boundary, we can't generate pins for it.
  • Some rural or unincorporated areas that aren't a recognized named community won't have a pin pack. A run in one of those areas still shows on your map and saves to your history, but it won't count toward any city's coverage.

Route syncing sometimes takes a few minutes, especially after a Watch-only workout, so give it time before assuming something's wrong. Don't delete the workout from Apple Fitness just because the route is missing.

If the route still hasn't appeared after a while, make sure your Apple Watch is near your iPhone (for Watch-only workouts) and open both StreetProof and the StreetProof Watch app; syncing happens when the two can talk. If that doesn't work, restarting both devices usually does the trick. Still missing? Email us.

Give it a few seconds, then close and reopen the app. If it still shows locked, open the upgrade screen and tap Restore Purchases, which re-checks your purchase with Apple and unlocks on the spot. If it's still stuck after that, email us and we'll sort it out.

Email hello@reforged.studio with a description of what happened. Including your iPhone model, iOS version, and what you were doing when it happened helps a lot.

Your data

Workouts are stored in Apple Health. Open the Fitness app or Health app to delete them; note that this is generally permanent and the workout cannot be recovered. Deletions there will be reflected in StreetProof.

With StreetProof+, yes: as GPX files, the standard route format most running apps and sites can read. Open Menu → History, tap the export button in the top corner, select the runs you want, and tap Export. Works for runs recorded in StreetProof and ones imported from Apple Health.

Workouts saved to Apple Health stay there and remain in the Fitness app. My Pins and app settings are removed with the app.

If you reinstall, your StreetProof+ purchase restores free (see the StreetProof+ section above), and your Proof Pin progress is rebuilt from your workout history during setup. The streets you've run stay proofed.
