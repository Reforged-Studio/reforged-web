Troubleshooting

A file won't import

When an import fails, FitFerry shows a specific reason rather than a generic error. The two most common:

  • No timestamps. Apple Health requires every workout to have a start and end time. Some files contain track points without timestamps, most often GPX routes exported as courses rather than recorded activities, and there is no honest way to invent times that were never recorded. Re-export the activity (not the route or course) from the original platform to get a timestamped file.
  • Unsupported format. FitFerry reads GPX, TCX, and FIT. Other formats, such as CSV or KML, need to be converted to one of those first. If an unsupported file is inside a zip alongside supported files, FitFerry simply skips it and imports the rest.

If you're unsure what a file contains, open it in the File Inspector first. It shows the dates, route points, and samples a file carries before anything is written to Health.

Where do imported workouts appear?

In the Health app under Browse → Activity → Workouts, and in the Fitness app on the Summary tab. Because imports keep their real historical dates, scroll back to the workout's original date: a 2021 ride appears in 2021, not today. Route maps and heart rate appear on the workout's detail page when the file included them.

FitFerry can't see Apple Health

Imports and exports need Health permission. Check Settings → Privacy & Security → Health → FitFerry, and make sure both read and write access are enabled for workouts.

Frequently asked

Where does my data go?

Only where you send it. Files are parsed on your device, and every read or write to Apple Health happens on your device. FitFerry has no servers, no accounts, and no analytics.

Which formats does it support?

GPX, TCX, and FIT for import. GPX and TCX for export. Zip archives containing those formats work too.

Does it work with a Strava or Garmin export zip?

Yes. Request your data export from the platform, get the zip onto your device, and FitFerry will import the workouts inside it.

Do imported workouts show up in Apple Fitness?

Yes. They appear in the Fitness and Health apps as real workouts at their real historical dates. A run from 2019 lands in 2019, with its route and heart rate intact.

Is it a subscription?

No. The unlock is a one-time $2.99 purchase. Use the free imports to see that everything works, then unlock when you're ready to bring the rest over.

How do I get my files onto my iPhone?

Any way you'd move a normal file: save them to iCloud Drive, AirDrop them from a Mac, or put them anywhere the Files app can reach. FitFerry opens them from there.
